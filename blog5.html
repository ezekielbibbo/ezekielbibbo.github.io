Project - Prosthetic Band

Phantom limb pain is a sudden, painful sensation experienced by many who have had a limb or part of a limb surgically removed. For amputees, these episodes can be both sporadic and can vary in discomfot. Due to a nervous system anomaly, the severed nerves relay signals to the brain, often interpreted as pain. Research indicates that vibration therapy can alleviate some of the pain during these episodes. However, the unpredictable nature of phantom limb pain makes data collection for studies challenging. As a researcher at XProjects, I collaborated with Dr. Goeran Fiedler to design a wearable device for amputees. This device, placed beneath a prosthetic socket, provides vibration. During the prototyping phase, I recognized that the circuitry and fit would be paramount. Consequently, I designed an initial band prototype to encircle a residual limb, delivering vibratory sensations from motors housed within the prosthesis. I eventually refined this to a final band prototype featuring powerful vibration motors powered by a LiPo battery. By the project's conclusion, we had provided two prototypes to an elated client.
