Project - Revolutionizing Mobility by Enhancing Power Chair Accessibility

Understanding the Challenge:
Power chair users, particularly those utilizing Group 2 power chairs, face a significant hurdle in their daily lives: the manual adjustment of their footplates. This task, essential for safe transfers to and from the chair, often poses a risk of injury and diminishes the user's independence and autonomy. Our journey to address this issue began with a deep dive into the lives of power chair users through an extensive ethnographic study at the Center for Assistive Technology (CAT). Our findings were further reinforced through dialogues with healthcare professionals, therapists, and the power chair user community.

Our Mission:
Confronted with this challenge, our team set out with a clear objective: to develop a footplate mechanism that can be controlled effortlessly by the user while seated. This endeavor aimed not just to solve a functional problem but to significantly enhance the quality of life for power chair users.



The Development Process:
Our journey to devise a solution has been both challenging and rewarding. It started with brainstorming sessions, leading to a series of innovative concepts. I came up with the concept of a sliding wheelchair mechanism, which can scoop under the feet and be moved using a hand-crank gear system. We then moved to the creation of low-resolution prototypes, using materials like foam core, available in the Pitt MakerSpace facilities. These prototypes were crucial in visualizing our ideas and understanding the practicalities of our designs.



Collaboration and Feedback:
Central to our development process has been our collaboration with healthcare professionals and the invaluable feedback from power chair users. Their insights have been instrumental in shaping our approach. We've hosted participatory sessions at the University of Pittsburgh’s Center for Assistive Technology, where team members were shown various classifications of assistive technology, focusing on the limitations of Group 2 chairs.



Challenges and Iterations:
Along the way, we faced numerous challenges. We scrutinized each design for potential hazards, user comfort, and feasibility. This iterative process, guided by feedback and expert consultations, helped us refine our ideas and move closer to a viable solution. Eventually, we closed in on the idea of the sliding footplate mechanism. Then, I began working on the first prototype.



Towards a Working Prototype:
After making a full CAD model of the design, I started working alongside the Swanson Center for Product Innovation (SCPI) to generate a mechanical frame and sliding footplate system. While we are yet in the process of developing a fully functional prototype, our commitment to creating a user-friendly, safe, and innovative footplate adjustment mechanism remains unwavering. Our design aims to be adaptable, fitting multiple brands of power chairs, and focused on enhancing user safety and autonomy.



Our Ongoing Commitment:
This project is more than just an academic endeavor; it's a commitment to improving the lives of those who rely on power chairs for mobility. We are dedicated to refining our design, addressing the challenges encountered, and ultimately providing a solution that truly makes a difference.



Stay tuned to our website for updates on our progress, insights from our journey, and the eventual unveiling of a solution that promises to transform the mobility experience for power chair users.
